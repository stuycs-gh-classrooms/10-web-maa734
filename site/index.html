The Scroll

The scroll is at the top of the violin and is curved holding all the strings. It keeps the strings tight and can have patterns on it sometimes.
Violin Scroll

The Fingerboard

The fingerboard is where the strings are placed on. It is the black piece that goes up to the scroll and where you place your fingers for different notes which make different sounds.
Violin Fingerboard

The Pegs

The pegs are on the scroll and are used to tune the violin. There are additional fine tuners on the tailpiece of thev violin which is connected to the shoulder rest. The fine tuners as the name suggests tune the violin more carefully whereas the pegs are more for general tuning and pitch.
Violin Pegs

The Bow

The bow is what a person uses to actually play the violin. When playing, the bow is placed between the fingerboard and the tailpiece to create sound.

The horse hair

The bow has horse hair which is rosened to make sound on the violin.
Violin Horse Hair

The frog

The frog is where a person holds the bow in a particular bow hold to play it.
Violin Frog
