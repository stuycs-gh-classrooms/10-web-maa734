The Koch Curve

Basic koch curve: 


            koch_curve(t, 4, 20)

This koch curve's modifications are:
1) At the end the turtle is turned to the right by 60 degrees causing the pattern to go over itself.
2) Additionally the colors are randomized in a specific range by changing the amount of red in the color.
3) Also the lengths of the koch curve are modified to give the specific shape of the pattern.


            koch_curve(t, 6, 50)
          


        def draw_koch(t, depth, length):
          t.pd()
          t.color(random.randrange(255), 30, 150)
          if depth == 1:
            t.fd(length)
          else:
            draw_koch(t, depth - 1, length)
            t.lt(60)
            draw_koch(t, depth - 1, length*.5)
            t.rt(120)
            draw_koch(t, depth - 1, length*.5)
            t.lt(60)
            draw_koch(t, depth - 1, length)
            t.rt(60)

The Sierpinski Triangle

Basic sierpinski triangle: 


              sierpinski(t, 5, 200)

The sierpinski's modifications are:
1) All of the angles in the recursion are swapped, making left right and right left.
2) The colors are also once again randomized in a specific range changing the amount of blue in the color.
3) Lastly, the turtle puts the pen up before completing the triangles, leaving empty spaces in the sierpinski triangle.


              sierpinski(t, 5, 300)

The Tree

Basic tree: 


                tree(t, 4, 50, 20)

The tree's modifications are:
1) The lengths of going forward and backwards in the intial and final steps are changed making them less.
2) The tree is also brown.
3) In each recursion the there is a randomized amount added to the angle.


                tree(t, 9, 70, 15)
              


            def tree(t, depth, length, angle):
                if depth == 1:
                    t.fd(length)
                    t.bk(length)
                    t.color(128,0,0)
                else:
                    t.fd(length/3)
                    t.rt(angle)
                    tree(t, depth-1, length, angle + random.randrange(-2,2))
                    t.lt(2*angle)
                    tree(t, depth-1, length, angle + random.randrange(-2,2))
                    t.rt(angle)
                    t.bk(length/3)
